History

I'm a lifelong tech lover who was pulled in a different career direction for the first decade of my professional life. Physical therapy provided me with a fulfilling way to help people and learn amazing new things. I met amaing patients and co-workers and can say that I am a better person for it. It was missing something though. Growth and challenge.

Outside of treating professional athletes, physical therapy is a relatively stable field. There are long delays between research results and their application in the field. Trust me, this is a good thing. You want a large body of evidence supporting a treatment method before implementing it. The upside of this is that it leads to stable, safe, and effective treatment protocols. The downside of this is that novel learning experiences are limited and problem solving is more human oriented than treatment oriented.

As I've gotten older I've begun to realize that what I desire in a career is continuous learning, problem solving, collaboration, and creation. I want to be paid to learn and contribute that knowledge to something bigger. I spent a year in the CS program in college and ultimately left because at that point in my life I could not handle not understanding everything immediately. It was the right choice at that point in my life, but as I have matured I have come to realize that the challenge is what makes it fulfilling. Being forced to admit you don't understand is how you grow and improve.

And so I came full circle bringing my career back to software and web development, a career I find more interesting and fulfilling the further down the rabbit hole I go.
