History

I'm a lifelong tech lover who was pulled in a different career direction for the first decade of my professional life. Physical therapy provided me with a fulfilling way to help people, make a difference, and have some amazing experiences. I was also fortunate to meet wonderful patients and co-workers along the way and can say that I am a better person for it. Ultimately, it was missing something though. Growth and challenge.

Outside of edge cases like treating professional athletes and performing research studies, physical therapy is a relatively stable field. There are long delays between research results and their application in the field. A number that was cited in school was 17 years from research results to widespread acceptance and application. Trust me, this is actually a good thing. You want a large body of evidence supporting a treatment method before implementing it. The upside of this is that it leads to stable, safe, and effective treatment protocols. The downside of this is that truly novel learning experiences are limited and problem solving is more human-oriented than treatment-oriented.

As I've matured, I've begun to realize that what I desire in a career is continuous learning, problem solving, collaboration, and creation. I want to learn and contribute that knowledge to something bigger, and ideally be compensated for my efforts. I spent time in the CS program in college and ultimately left because I could not handle not how foreign everything was. I felt like I was behind from the start being surrounded by people who had been programming since they were young. It was the right choice at that point in my life for many reasons, but as I have matured I have come to realize that the challenge is what makes it fulfilling. Being forced to admit you don't understand is how you grow and improve.

And so I come full circle, bringing my career back to software and web development. It is a career I that I find more interesting and fulfilling each day as I wander further down the rabbit hole. There are lifetimes of knowledge and learning here and that's just what already exists. Tomorrow will bring another lifetime, and I'm ready to go.
